This is a risk and decision-governance artifact. It does not itself approve legal, clinical, regulatory, financial, privacy, security, operational, design, or accessibility risk. Risk scoring is qualitative and prioritization-oriented. Risk scores are not statistical predictions. Vendor and partner dependencies remain unselected unless prior evidence proves otherwise.


## Risk Governance

A risk is an uncertain event or condition with material consequence. Cause is the reason it may occur. Consequence is the harm if it occurs. Control is an existing preventive or detective measure. Mitigation reduces likelihood or impact. Contingency is the response if mitigation fails. Early warning is evidence that risk is increasing. Inherent, current, and residual risk are tracked separately. Risk acceptance requires authorized human approval; Codex cannot accept risk. Launch, capability, expansion, release, and Phase 1 blockers remain blockers until approved evidence changes them.

## Risk Scoring Model

Likelihood ordinals are RARE=1, UNLIKELY=2, POSSIBLE=3, LIKELY=4, ALMOST-CERTAIN=5. Impact ordinals are MINOR=1, MODERATE=2, MAJOR=3, SEVERE=4, CATASTROPHIC=5. Current score equals current likelihood ordinal times current impact ordinal. Residual score equals residual likelihood ordinal times residual impact ordinal. Scores order review priority only and are not statistical probability, frequency, monetary loss, or approval. Top-ten ties are broken by higher impact, pilot-blocking status, patient-safety/privacy significance, and dependency criticality.

## Blocking Classification and Treatment Values

Blocking classifications are PHASE-1-BLOCKER, PILOT-LAUNCH-BLOCKER, CAPABILITY-LAUNCH-BLOCKER, EXPANSION-BLOCKER, RELEASE-BLOCKER, NONBLOCKING-BUT-MONITORED, and FUTURE-SCOPE. Treatments are AVOID, REDUCE, TRANSFER, ACCEPT-PENDING-AUTHORITY, MONITOR, DEFER-CAPABILITY, PAUSE-CAPABILITY, BLOCK-LAUNCH, and REQUIRE-CONTINGENCY. Codex does not use ACCEPTED-BY-AUTHORITY, AVOIDED, TRANSFERRED, or CLOSED without approval evidence.
