## Identity and Authority Verification

Own-account, lost-account, guardian, clinical-proxy, authorized representative, employer, HMO, sponsor, court/regulator, and law-enforcement requests each require identity and authority checks. No employer, HMO, sponsor, family payer, or guardian may use a DSR workflow to obtain data outside approved authority.

## Access Response

Access responses require data inventory, third-party redaction, clinical context, audit-history boundaries, security-data restrictions, provider-confidentiality questions, approved format, secure delivery, expiry, and audit.

## Correction

Correction distinguishes demographic, contact, identity, clinical amendment, prescription correction, result correction, audit annotation, financial correction, and provider credential correction. Finalized clinical information uses amendments or corrected versions.

## Deletion or Erasure

Deletion decisions distinguish eligible deletion, restriction instead of deletion, clinical/legal/financial/security/audit retention conflicts, legal hold, de-identification, relationship termination, account closure, processor deletion, backup handling, and decision explanation.

## Portability and Export

Exports require subject and scope verification, format review, clinical/financial/audit/security boundaries, third-party redaction, provider-detail authorization, encryption, download expiry, and audit.

## No Direct Database Editing

DSR completion must use approved correction, export, restriction, deletion, de-identification, and audit mechanisms. Operations must not directly edit production records.

## Future DSR Tests

Synthetic tests must cover verified request, failed identity verification, guardian request, authorized representative, wrong patient, cross-tenant attempt, third-party redaction, clinical amendment, erasure conflict, legal hold, restriction, portability, processor coordination, audit, secure delivery, and appeal.
