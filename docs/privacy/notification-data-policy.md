## Purpose

Define data allowed in in-application notifications, email, SMS, push, lock-screen previews, approved messaging channels, internal operations notifications, and provider notifications.

## Notification Principles

- Minimum necessary.
- Neutral wording by default.
- No sensitive content in subject lines or lock-screen previews.
- Authenticated application required for detailed information.
- No provider location before authorized disclosure.
- No clinical record in a notification payload.
- No reusable sensitive tokens.
- No unnecessary patient names.
- No broad recipient lists.
- Delivery does not equal clinical acknowledgment.

## Notification Classifications

ESSENTIAL-SERVICE, CLINICAL-SAFETY, SECURITY, FINANCIAL, OPERATIONAL, OPTIONAL-PRODUCT, and MARKETING are conceptual classifications, not final legal categories.

## Deep Links

Deep links require authenticated destination, expiring or narrowly scoped token where used, no sensitive data in URL, no diagnosis in path, no provider address in path, no raw order or patient identifiers where avoidable, authorization recomputation after opening, safe failure state, and audit where sensitive.

## Critical and Emergency Notifications

Critical and emergency notifications require clinically approved wording, minimum necessary content, approved multi-channel handling, delivery status, explicit acknowledgment where required, failed-contact escalation, no assumption that push or SMS delivery proves patient receipt, and audit.

## Accessibility

Notifications must use plain language, screen-reader compatible content, text alternatives, language preference where approved, avoidance of ambiguous icons, accessible action links, and support for communication needs.

## Future Notification Tests

Synthetic tests must cover generic appointment, payment, and result-available messages; no diagnosis, medicine name, result value, or protected provider address; safe lock-screen preview; authenticated deep link; expired deep link; wrong actor; wrong tenant; and accessibility.
