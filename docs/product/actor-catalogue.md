## Conceptual Definitions

### Core person-level concepts

- `Person`: the real-world individual.
- `UserAccount`: an authentication principal used to access the product.
- `Patient`: a continuity care identity linked to one Person.
- `Prospective patient`: a person in pre-Patient creation state.
- `Practitioner`: clinician or medically qualified actor exercising clinical intent.
- `Staff member`: operational or clinical actor assigned to an organization/facility.
- `Organization member`: a Person participating in an organization context.
- `Facility member`: a Person acting inside a specific facility context.
- `Role`: context-driven permissions and duties.
- `Plan member`: a Person enrolled under a plan, contract, or sponsor arrangement.
- `Beneficiary`: Person whose care episode receives payer-supported funding.
- `Dependent`: Person receiving support under another person/program relationship.
- `Payer`: actor with funding authority.
- `Sponsor`: actor who supports care financing without clinical rights by default.
- `Guardian`: actor with legally constrained delegation.
- `Clinical proxy`: adult delegate with explicit clinical authorization.
- `Caregiver`: non-clinical support actor with scoped tasks.
- `Administrator`: role that runs operational or support functions, not automatic full access.

### Organization actors

- `Employer`, `HMO`, `Provider organization`, `Pharmacy organization`, `Laboratory organization`, `Hospital/referral organization`, `Home-care agency`, `NelyoHealth operations organization`, `Delivery partner`.

## Actor classification rules

1. A single person can hold multiple actor rows as they move through lifecycle events.
2. A role applies only inside an explicit organization or care context.
3. Membership and role are independent; membership alone does not grant care rights.
4. Context switching must always be explicit and user-visible.
5. Organization membership does not merge patient identity.
6. Facility operations do not replace organization membership rules.
7. No actor receives unrestricted clinical access solely due to admin, payer, or sponsor title.

## Actor lifecycle (conceptual)

The actor lifecycle applies to all human and organization actors at a conceptual level:

- `Invited`: entity or person is known but inactive.
- `Pending verification`: required credentials/identity or authority checks are in progress.
- `Active`: actor can perform scoped actions in approved context.
- `Suspended`: temporary operational restriction with audit trail.
- `Offboarded`: persistent removal of active roles from context.
- `Expired`: authority/certification ended by date or contract.
- `Revoked`: explicit authority withdrawal for safety, fraud, or compliance.

No lifecycle transition is permitted to silently grant access across contexts without explicit re-authorization.
